Based on what the app does (protecting PII before sending to AI), here are the suggestions I'd prioritise:

1.==High impact — core use case improvements
Clipboard auto-protect — A "Paste & Protect" button that grabs whatever is on the clipboard, 
masks it instantly, and puts the protected version back on the clipboard. No typing needed. 
This is the most natural workflow.

2.==More PII types auto-detected — Currently only emails, phones, and custom terms. Should also catch:
Physical addresses
Credit card numbers
National ID / passport numbers
Dates of birth
Bank account numbers
3.==Protection profiles / templates — Pre-built sets for different contexts. E.g. Medical (mask patient names, DOB, NHS numbers), 
Legal (mask client names, case numbers), HR (mask employee names, salaries). One click switches the whole ruleset.
Custom regex patterns — Power users (developers, lawyers) often need to mask things emails/phones won't catch. 
Let them define their own pattern e.g. INV-\d{5} for invoice numbers.


Trust and compliance — makes it serious for business use
5.==Audit log — A tab showing every masking session: date, how many items protected, what types. 
Important for GDPR/HIPAA compliance — companies need to prove they protected data.

6.==App lock / PIN — Lock the app after idle time. If someone walks up to an unlocked screen they shouldn't see stored chat history.


Team use — unlocks B2B revenue
7.==Shared company terms list — You already have company-sync.js. A team admin defines the protected terms (client names, project codenames, internal systems) and everyone on the team gets them automatically. This is the feature that turns it from a personal tool into a company tool.
Biggest single feature I'd add first: The Clipboard auto-protect — it removes all friction. Right now the user has to open the app, type or paste, then copy. With clipboard monitoring it becomes: copy from anywhere → click one button → paste protected text into AI. That's the killer workflow.
